## Phal Card (outcome)

At session end, the **Phal Card** reflects the *result*: files changed (and how many
were tests), which validations were detected, and a readiness level — from
*Informational* through *Needs Review* to *High Risk*. It's **task-aware**: a docs
change isn't judged for missing tests.

## Dashboard

A calm, read-only mirror of your practice:

- **At a glance** — your self-comparative Karma trend (it stays a quiet "forming"
  note until you have a few sessions).
- **Validation consistency** — a contribution-graph-style strip of recent sessions.
- **Heatmap** — where you validate (task type × validation).
- **High-risk watchlist** — sessions worth revisiting.
- **Why did my Karma move?** — an honest diff of what changed since last time.

## Karma Card (shareable)

Generate a **Karma Card** — a printable/shareable certificate of your validation
practice (e.g. *"Luminous Validator — I write with AI, and I verify every line"*),
with your validation rate, best streak, and Karma. It's generated **100% locally**.

> The same card engine powers the web [Validation Dojo](/start/introduction/) — earn
> a card there, then make it real in your editor.
