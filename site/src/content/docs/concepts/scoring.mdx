Your **Karma Score** (0–100) is **objective**. It's built only from validation
actions that were observed or logged — never from feeling-based self-report. It
answers one thing: *did you verify the AI's work before trusting it?*

## Design principles

- **Action-based.** Points come from real validations (tests, build, lint, type
  check) you actually ran — not from how much AI you used.
- **Validation-dominated.** ~90 of 100 points are validation; a soft prompt-clarity
  hint contributes at most 10.
- **Traceable.** Every point maps to a named rule, so the score is never a black box.
- **Fair (no vacuous truth).** A check only earns points if it actually *ran* — you
  can't score "clean" by simply never testing.

## Self-comparative trend

The headline number you see over time is a self-comparative **EMA** — you against
*your own* rolling average, not a global leaderboard. With only a few sessions it
stays a calm "forming" note rather than grading a sparse history.
